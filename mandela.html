// INTRODUCTION //

The Mandela Effect is the phenomenon where a large group of people share the same false memory. Named after the widespread false belief that Nelson Mandela died in prison in the 1980s. The phenomenon gained viral attention in 2015, when thousands of people swore they remembered the Berenstein Bears — not the Berenstain Bears. Multiple realities can explain this. Confabulation. Quantum immortality. CERN opening a portal in 2012. Or just bad memory. The lab below documents the most documented cases — and lets you test your own.
